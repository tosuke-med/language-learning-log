Today, I read an article about English writing using LLMs. This article is interesting because it highlights some important points about using LLMs to write in English.

[https://theconversation.com/how-ai-english-and-human-english-differ-and-how-to-decide-when-to-use-artificial-language-277455](https://theconversation.com/how-ai-english-and-human-english-differ-and-how-to-decide-when-to-use-artificial-language-277455)

LLMs are typically trained on large amounts of internet text, and their output tends to reflect an average of that data. Therefore, if we do not provide specific prompts, the generated text may not be suitable for our purpose.

This is especially important in research fields, where each domain has highly specialized terminology and expressions. If the LLM has not been guided or fine-tuned for such language, it can be difficult to produce appropriate academic writing.

This article explains that situation very clearly.
